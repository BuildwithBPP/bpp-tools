BPP Sales Qualification

Ideal Client Profiles

Three distinct client types. Three distinct packages. Use this to qualify every lead before a proposal is written. If the client doesn't match one of these profiles clearly, don't force it.

Tier 1 — Launch Pad
"The Growth-Ready Founder"
Building something real — just needs the foundation to do it right
Package $1,500–$2,000 Launch Pad · Upgrade target within 12 mo
Who they are
  • Business age: 0–18 months
  • Solo operator or 1–3 person team
  • Revenue: Pre-revenue to $150K
  • Industries: Trades, cleaning, consulting, creatives, local services, wellness
  • Often first-generation business owner
What actually hurts
  • "I don't look like a real business yet"
  • No legal structure — worried about liability
  • No website or online presence at all
  • Overwhelmed — doesn't know what to do first
  • Losing credibility with clients or lenders
What they value
  • Speed — wants to be open for business now
  • Simplicity — not overwhelmed with complexity
  • Credibility — looking legitimate and professional
  • Clarity — someone telling them what to do first
  • Affordability — price sensitive at this stage
What they sound like
"I've been running this out of my personal account. I need to make this official and get a real website before I can go after bigger clients."
Where to find them Google Search Referrals (family, accountants) Instagram / TikTok Local chambers YouTube
Green lights — close it
  • No LLC or EIN yet — clear need
  • No website at all or just a social page
  • Has a real service to sell, just not set up
  • Ambitious — talks about where they want to be
  • Shows clear Operator potential within 12 months
Red flags — walk away
  • Only wants a business plan, nothing else
  • Budget under $1,000 — wrong fit
  • No real service or product to sell yet
  • Side hustle mentality, no growth ambition
  • No upgrade potential toward Operator
Tier 2 — Operator System ⭐ Primary Focus
"The Established Operator"
Revenue is real — but everything still runs through the owner
Package $4,500 Operator System · BPP's primary target
Who they are
  • Business age: 3–8 years
  • Team: 5–25 employees or contractors
  • Revenue: $250K–$2M annually
  • Industries: Cleaning, trades, landscaping, healthcare, agencies, professional services
  • Owner-operated — founder still runs everything
What actually hurts
  • "Everything runs through me — I can't step away"
  • Leads fall through the cracks constantly
  • Tools exist but don't talk to each other
  • Client onboarding is inconsistent every time
  • Can't trust the numbers — no real reporting
What they value
  • Outcomes over features — "show me what changes"
  • Proof — a case study from someone like them
  • Trust — they buy from people they believe in
  • Clarity — know exactly what they're getting
  • Respect — they built this, want to be treated as such
What they sound like
"I have a CRM — I think. But nobody uses it. And I'm still the one chasing every lead and walking every new client through everything myself. I can't keep doing this."
Psychographic profile
  • Identity: "I built this myself" — deep pride in what they've created
  • Core fear: Losing what they built, chaos getting worse as they grow
  • Core drive: Wants to step back without things falling apart
  • Relationship to tech: Open to it but skeptical — has been burned before
  • Buying trigger: Referral from someone they trust, or an industry case study
How they buy
  • Trusts referrals above everything — warm intro beats any cold pitch
  • Does own research first — 60–70% decided before first call
  • Needs proof of outcome, not a feature list
  • Decision-maker is usually the owner — no committee
  • Hesitates on price when really hesitating on trust
Where to find them Accountant referrals Bookkeeper referrals Google Search LinkedIn Chamber events Peer owner referrals
Green lights — close it
  • Says "everything runs through me" in some version
  • Has tools but doesn't trust or use them
  • Revenue growing but ops getting messier
  • Open to tech when they understand the outcome
  • Came through a warm referral
Red flags — walk away
  • Under $150K revenue — not an Operator yet
  • Resistant to systems entirely
  • Pushes hard on price before understanding value
  • No urgency — pain isn't painful enough yet
  • Won't commit to adopting what gets built
Tier 3 — Scale System
"The Scale-Ready Executive"
High revenue, real infrastructure — but decisions are reactive and data is scattered
Package $10K–$15K Scale System · Via referral or upgrade
Who they are
  • Business age: 5+ years
  • Team: 15–50 employees, multi-location
  • Revenue: $1.5M–$5M annually
  • Industries: Healthcare, professional services, janitorial, logistics, agencies
  • Already has tools — CRM, QB, website all exist
What actually hurts
  • "I have revenue but I don't have clarity"
  • Data is scattered — no single source of truth
  • Decisions are reactive, not data-driven
  • Leadership depends on owner for answers
  • Can't see margin by service, location, or client
Key differences from Operator
  • Already has CRM — BPP optimizes, doesn't install
  • Already has a website — BPP enhances, rarely rebuilds
  • Already has QB — BPP connects and builds from it
  • Needs BI and financial planning, not basic reporting
  • Decision may involve leadership team, not just owner
What they sound like
"We're doing $2M a year but I still don't really know which part of the business is actually profitable. My team is running around and I'm still the one answering all the questions."
Where to find them CPA / banker referrals Operator upgrades LinkedIn authority content Speaking engagements Executive peer groups
Green lights — close it
  • "We have data but I can't see it in one place"
  • Has all the tools but they're disconnected
  • Leadership asks owner for answers constantly
  • Revenue growing but margin visibility is zero
  • Came through a high-trust referral (CPA, banker)
Red flags — walk away
  • No existing tech stack — they're an Operator
  • Leadership resistant to changing operations
  • Expects Scale output at Operator pricing
  • No internal champion to drive adoption
  • Decision-maker unclear after two conversations

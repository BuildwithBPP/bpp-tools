Role Guide
The deal owner model + who owns what by default. Assignments confirmed at each kickoff.
Deal Owner — whoever closes the deal
  • Primary client contact for the full engagement
  • Runs all client-facing touchpoints — calls, check-ins, updates
  • Collects all three payments (deposit, midpoint, final)
  • Logs all client interactions in HubSpot within 24 hrs
  • Updates deal stage within 24 hrs of any change
  • Sends completion summary, survey, testimonial ask, referral ask
  • Owns the client relationship even after project closes
Tech Lead — Daunte
  • Primary owner of AI workflow automation (Make, Zapier, custom builds)
  • Owns all BI dashboard builds (Power BI, Looker Studio)
  • Owns financial planning analysis and QB data connections
  • Head of strategy — final technical sign-off before client delivery
  • Most technically versatile — fills gaps where needed
  • Internal financial operations and partner distributions
Any Partner — shared delivery pool
  • Business plan writing — assigned at kickoff by bandwidth
  • Google Business Profile setup and optimization
  • SOP documentation for client processes
  • Training sessions — whoever built the deliverable trains on it
  • Loom recordings for any deliverable they own
  • LLC formation and EIN registration
Specialized skills by partner
  • Eli — Marketing lead: content, brand, website delivery (Wix, Webflow), client experience
  • Kenny — CRM setup and optimization (HubSpot), sales support, business planning, training documentation
  • Daunte — AI workflows, BI dashboards, financial analysis, strategy oversight, fills wherever needed
  • Exact assignments confirmed in internal scope call at each project kickoff

Tier 1
Launch Pad
2–3 weeks · $1,500–$2,000 · Growth-Ready Founder
Target client0–18 months · Solo to 3-person team
Initiation Execution Monitoring Closure
Discovery call — what to ask
Qualify the client
  • What does your business do and who do you serve?
  • Are you structured as an LLC or sole proprietor yet?
  • Do you have an EIN?
  • Are you generating revenue or still pre-revenue?
  • Do you have a website or any online presence right now?
  • What's the #1 thing you need to feel like a real business?
Understand the goal
  • Where do you want to be in 12 months?
  • Do you have a logo or brand identity, or starting from zero?
  • Who are your first customers — how are you finding them now?
  • What's your timeline — any urgency to get this done?
  • Have you worked with a consultant or agency before?
Close it — green lights
  • No LLC or EIN yet
  • No website at all or just a social page
  • Has a real service to sell, just not set up legally
  • Shows Operator ambition — wants to grow beyond this
  • Realistic on 2–3 week timeline
Walk away — red flags
  • Only wants a business plan, nothing else
  • Budget under $1,000 — wrong fit
  • No real service or product to sell yet
  • Side hustle mentality, no growth ambition
  • No upgrade potential toward Operator within 12 months

Operator System
6–8 weeks · $4,500 flat · Established Operator
Target client$250K–$2M · 5–25 employees
Initiation Planning Execution Monitoring Closure
Discovery call — what to ask
Diagnose the operation
  • Walk me through how a new lead comes in — from first contact to signed contract?
  • What falls through the cracks most often in your business?
  • Do you have a CRM? Which one — does your team actually use it?
  • How do you onboard a new client right now — is it consistent every time?
  • If you were unavailable for 2 weeks, what would break?
  • How do you currently track revenue or profitability by service?
Understand the business
  • How many people on your team — employees vs contractors?
  • What are your top 2–3 services by revenue?
  • What tools are you already paying for?
  • What's the one thing that if fixed would have the biggest impact?
  • What does success look like 6 months from now?
  • What's your investment comfort level for this engagement?
Close it — green lights
  • Says "everything runs through me" in some version
  • Has tools but doesn't trust or use them consistently
  • Revenue is real and growing but ops are messier as it grows
  • Open to tech when they understand the outcome
  • Warm referral — trust is already partially built
Walk away — red flags
  • Under $150K revenue — not an Operator yet
  • Resistant to systems entirely
  • Pushes hard on price before understanding value
  • No urgency — pain isn't painful enough yet
  • Won't commit to adopting what gets built

Tier 3
Scale System
10–14 weeks · $10,000–$15,000 · Scale-Ready Executive
Target client$1.5M–$5M · 15–50 employees
Initiation Planning Execution Monitoring Closure
Discovery call — what to ask
Diagnose the operation
  • If I asked right now what your most profitable service is, could you prove it with data?
  • How many tools is your team using that don't talk to each other?
  • When leadership needs to make a decision, where does that information come from?
  • What processes still require someone to manually move information between systems?
  • What would you do with your time if operational questions stopped coming to you?
Understand the vision
  • What does growth look like — more locations, more services, more clients?
  • Do you have a leadership team that can run areas without you?
  • Have you worked with consultants before — what worked, what didn't?
  • What's the investment you're prepared to make to get this right?
  • Who else needs to be involved in the decision to move forward?
Close it — green lights
  • Has revenue but no financial clarity or data visibility
  • Already has CRM + website + QB — needs intelligence on top
  • Leadership still dependent on owner for answers
  • Came through CPA, banker, or high-trust referral
  • Talks in outcomes and ROI — understands investment
Walk away — red flags
  • No existing tech stack — they're an Operator, not Scale
  • Leadership resistant to changing how they operate
  • Expects Scale output at Operator pricing
  • No internal champion to drive adoption post-delivery
  • Decision-maker unclear after two conversations

Shared Standards
Rules that apply to every engagement, every partner, every time — no exceptions.
Scope protection
  • Everything delivered is documented in the proposal — no verbal promises
  • Any work outside original scope requires a written change order before starting
  • If a client asks for more than scoped, that's a change order — not a favor
  • Never discount a package after a proposal is sent
  • Alert the full team when a project hits 80% of estimated hours
Communication standards
  • Respond to all client messages within 24 hours on business days
  • Weekly status update to client — even if nothing changed, send a note
  • All client communication logged in HubSpot — not in personal DMs or texts
  • Support channel: email + Microsoft Teams. Not WhatsApp or personal phone.
  • If a deadline slips, tell the client before the deadline — never after
Payment enforcement
  • Launch Pad: 50% at signing · 50% within 5 days of client sign-off on all deliverables
  • Operator: 50% at signing · 25% when process map + CRM delivered · 25% within 5 days of final sign-off
  • Scale: 40% at signing · 30% when BI dashboard + automations delivered · 30% within 5 days of full playbook sign-off
  • Late payment: work pauses automatically after 7 days overdue — written in every contract, no exceptions
  • All invoices created in QuickBooks — set auto-reminders: 3 days before due + day of due date
  • 5-day acceptance window on all deliverables — if client does not respond, milestone is treated as accepted and payment is due
Quality gates
  • Every deliverable reviewed internally before going to the client
  • Every workflow tested with live or simulated data before handoff
  • Every website change QA'd on mobile + desktop before publishing
  • Every Loom watched back before sharing — confirm audio is clear
  • Daunte reviews all technical deliverables (AI, BI, financial) before client delivery
HubSpot hygiene — Deal Owner
  • Every new lead entered in HubSpot within 24 hours of first contact
  • Deal stage updated within 24 hours of any status change
  • Discovery call notes logged in contact record immediately after the call
  • Every closed-won deal: create Monday project, assign partners, set kickoff date
  • Every closed-lost deal: log reason in HubSpot — needed for ICP and pricing learning
Post-delivery — every package
  • Send upgrade credit reminder in closeout email — plant the seed early
  • Request testimonial or Google review 2 weeks after delivery
  • Add client to quarterly check-in calendar if they opted in
  • Flag every completed client for referral outreach at 30 days post-delivery
  • Log project learnings in Notion — what took longer, what to do differently next time
